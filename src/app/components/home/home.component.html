Professional Profile

Dr Peter McKavanagh MB BCh BAO, MRCP(UK), MRCP(Ire), MMedSc, PhD

Peter is a dedicated and enthusiastic consultant Cardiologist working at the Ulster and Royal Victoria Hospitals in Belfast. Areas of interests include general and acute cardiology, coronary artery disease, interventional cardiology (angioplasty/stenting), cardiac CT, heart failure, cholesterol management and medical education. All three of his degrees were from Queen's University Belfast. His primary Medical Degree with distinction was in 2003; his Master in Medical Education with distinction was in 2010; and his PhD in Cardiac CT was in 2016. He has a keen interest in research having published numerous papers and given international presentations. He was also runner up in the European Society of Cardiology Young Investigator Award in 2013. His general cardiology training was in Belfast and he completed the membership examinations for the Royal College of Physicians for both the UK and Ireland. Peter then had a two-year fellowship at a large cardiology centre in Toronto and completed a diploma in Interventional Cardiology from the Royal College of Canadian Cardiologists during this time. He also has an interest in third world cardiology, having spent 6 months volunteering in Kenya in a large cardiology centre in Nairobi, as well being involved with the Save a Heart Cardiac Surgery Nigerian Mission. Peter has regular cardiology clinics and is happy to see all adult cardiology complaints.

Personal profile

Peter has always had a keen interest in sport, and continues to train regularly. He was captain of the Queen’s University Belfast Judo Club during this undergraduate degree and was part of the team that won five consecutive Irish University titles. He also represented Northern Ireland at junior and senior levels. He has also volunteered with the Belfast Trust homeless service and with vulnerable adults.

 

Patient information (3 drop down pages)

Symptoms

Cardiovascular disease remains one of the most significant causes of death in Northern Ireland. In 2015 27.4% of all deaths in the UK were caused by cardiovascular disease. Common cardiology symptoms include chest pain, palpitation, shortness of breath, dizziness, blackout/collapses. These are often seen with common cardiology conditions such as:

  • Angina
  • Aortic Stenosis and other valvular conditions
  • Atrial Fibrillation (AF) and other cardiac arrhythmias
  • Heart Failure
  • High Blood Pressure
  • High Cholesterol
  • Heart Block
  • Cardiomyopathy
  • Vasovagal Syncope

Dr McKavanagh is happy to answer any questions that you may have. For information about any additional conditions not featured within the site, please contact us for more information. Patients can self-refer but we do recommend input from your own GP to help with ensuring you are dealt with appropriately.

Investigations and Management

To help with diagnosis, some cardiology tests may be necessary. These may include

  • Electrocardiogram (ECG) (an electrical tracing of the rhythm heart)
  • Echocardiography (an ultrasound of the heart)
  • 24HR Blood Pressure Monitoring (a device to assess blood pressure over 24 hours)
  • Exercise Stress Test (EST) (a treadmill test to assess your ECG and Blood Pressure when the heart is stressed)
  • Coronary Angiography (an invasive dye test to assess coronary arteries for blockages)
  • Ambulatory ECG (a monitoring device to assess the heart rhythm over a period of time)
  • Stress Echocardiography (an ultrasound test to assess the heart when it is stressed)
  • Cardiac magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) (a specialised scan of the heart that is useful for assessing the heart muscle)
  • Cardiac computerised tomography (CT) (a specialised scan of the heart that is useful for assessing coronary arteries for calcium and narrowing)

    • For many patients, no further treatment is required after the initial comprehensive assessment. However, some patients do require:

    • Coronary Angioplasty/Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI)/ Stenting
    • Pacemaker
    • Implantable Defibrillator
    • Pressure Wire Testing
    • Electrophysiology Studies
    • Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting
    • Valve surgery/intervention
